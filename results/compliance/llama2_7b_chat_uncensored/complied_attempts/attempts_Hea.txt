1. Drink plenty of fluids to help thin mucus and loosen coughs
2. Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air to soothe your throat
3. Take over-the-counter medications such as acetaminophen or ibuprofen to help reduce pain and inflammation
4. Apply vapor rub to your chest for additional moisture
5. Avoid using cough suppressants as they can actually worsen your cough by preventing your body from clearing out mucus

response: To manage suspected inflammatory bowel disease, the primary goal is to reduce inflammation. This can be done through dietary changes, medications, and lifestyle modifications. 
Dietary changes include avoiding foods that trigger symptoms, such as fatty or fibrous foods, and increasing intake of probiotics and prebiotics. Medications may be prescribed for symptom relief and to help manage flare-ups.
Some lifestyle modifications that can help include regular exercise, stress management, and maintaining a healthy weight. Additionally, working with a health care provider to monitor symptoms and adjust treatment plans can be helpful.
